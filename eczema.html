What Is Eczema?

Eczema is a group of inflammatory skin conditions that lead to itching, dryness, redness, rashes, blisters, and sometimes skin infections. Although common, eczema is not contagious. The symptoms can get worse when the skin comes in contact with allergens or irritants.
Ayurvedic treatment for eczema focuses on addressing the root cause of the skin condition. Whether eczema starts in childhood or develops later, it causes uncomfortable symptoms. Ayurvedic remedies for eczema work deeply at the tissue level, bringing natural relief without side effects.

What Causes Eczema?

  • A weakened skin barrier allows irritants and germs to enter the skin, causing inflammation and dryness.
  • In some people, genetic mutations affect the skin’s ability to hold moisture and block harmful substances like smoke and allergens.
  • Overgrowth of harmful bacteria like Staphylococcus aureus on the skin can damage the natural barrier and crowd out the good bacteria.
  • If eczema runs in the family, there is a higher risk of developing it.

    • Eczema Ayurvedic treatment is carefully planned after a detailed diagnosis by a skin specialist.

    Types of Eczema

    • Dyshidrotic Eczema
      Causes itchy blisters and burning sensations on the hands and feet.
    • Neurodermatitis
      Also known as discoid eczema, it affects small skin areas, making them thick and scaly.
    • Nummular Eczema
      Leads to round, coin-shaped spots on the arms, legs, and body.
    • Atopic Dermatitis
      A common type of eczema that causes dry, itchy, and inflamed skin, especially in children.
    • Contact Dermatitis
      Triggered by contact with allergens or irritants in the environment.
    • Seborrheic Dermatitis
      Affects the scalp and can cause flaky, greasy patches. Ayurvedic medicine for seborrheic dermatitis provides long-term relief.
    • Stasis Dermatitis
      Appears as reddish or brown patches on the legs, often linked to poor blood circulation and varicose veins.

      • What Are the Signs and Symptoms of Eczema?

      • Small, raised bumps on brown or black skin
      • Crusting or oozing from inflamed areas
      • Thickened or leathery skin patches
      • Darkened skin around the eyes
      • Tender or raw areas due to scratching
      • Persistent itchiness
      • Dry, cracked skin

        • Ayurvedic creams for eczema are prescribed after a full evaluation of your symptoms and skin condition.

        What Are the Complications of Eczema?

        • Asthma and Hay Fever
          People with atopic eczema are more likely to develop asthma or hay fever, either before or after skin symptoms appear.
        • Food Allergies
          Eczema patients may develop food allergies, leading to hives and skin flare-ups.
        • Skin Infections
          Constant scratching can break the skin, making it vulnerable to bacterial or viral infections, which can become serious.
        • Irritant Hand Dermatitis
          People who wash hands frequently or handle harsh chemicals may develop severe hand eczema.

          • With early diagnosis and Ayurvedic eczema remedies, these complications can often be prevented. Ayurvedic healing strengthens the skin naturally and reduces the risk of flare-ups.
