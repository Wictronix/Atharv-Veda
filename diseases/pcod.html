What Is PCOD?

Polycystic Ovary Disease (PCOD) is a common hormonal disorder affecting women of reproductive age. It occurs when the ovaries produce an excess of androgens (male hormones), leading to irregular ovulation and the development of cysts on the ovaries. While the exact cause is not fully understood, factors such as insulin resistance, low-grade inflammation, and genetic predisposition are believed to contribute to the development of PCOD.
Women with PCOD may experience symptoms like irregular menstrual cycles, weight gain, acne, and excessive hair growth. If left untreated, PCOD can lead to complications such as infertility, type 2 diabetes, and metabolic syndrome.

Types of PCOD

PCOD can present in different forms. The main types include:

  • Insulin-Resistant PCOD: Linked to insulin resistance and often associated with weight gain.
  • Inflammatory PCOD: Related to chronic inflammation in the body.
  • Adrenal PCOD: Caused by excess production of androgens from the adrenal glands, often triggered by stress.
  • Post-Pill PCOD: Occurs after stopping oral contraceptive pills, leading to temporary hormonal imbalance.
