What Is Jaundice?

Jaundice, also known as Kamala in Ayurveda, is a condition characterized by the yellowing of the skin, mucous membranes, and the whites of the eyes. This yellowish hue occurs due to an excess of bilirubin in the blood. Bilirubin is a yellow-orange substance produced during the normal breakdown of red blood cells. The liver processes bilirubin, but if the liver is diseased or the bile ducts are obstructed, bilirubin can accumulate, leading to jaundice.
In Ayurveda, jaundice is considered a disorder of the pitta dosha, which governs metabolism and transformation in the body. An imbalance in pitta, often due to liver dysfunction, leads to the accumulation of toxins and excess bile, resulting in jaundice.

Types of Jaundice

  • Pre-hepatic Jaundice: Caused by excessive breakdown of red blood cells before the liver processes bilirubin.
  • Hepatic Jaundice: Results from liver dysfunction or disease, affecting the liver’s ability to process bilirubin.
  • Post-hepatic Jaundice: Occurs when bile ducts are blocked, preventing bilirubin from leaving the liver.
  • Neonatal Jaundice: Common in newborns due to immature liver function, usually temporary.
