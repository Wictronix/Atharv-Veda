What Is Arthritis?

Arthritis is a condition characterized by inflammation and stiffness in the joints, leading to pain and reduced mobility. In Ayurveda, arthritis is often linked to an imbalance in the body's doshas, particularly Vata. The accumulation of toxins (ama) and impaired digestion are believed to exacerbate the condition. This imbalance can result from factors such as poor diet, lack of exercise, and aging.

Types of Arthritis

  • Osteoarthritis: The most common type, affecting cartilage between joints. Causes pain and stiffness as cartilage wears down. Ayurveda focuses on pain relief and flexibility.
  • Psoriatic Arthritis: Occurs in people with psoriasis. Ayurvedic remedies aim to reduce both skin and joint symptoms.
  • Rheumatoid Arthritis: An autoimmune disorder attacking joint lining. Ayurveda helps reduce inflammation and improve mobility.
  • Septic Arthritis: Caused by infection spreading to joints. Ayurveda supports recovery by boosting immunity and removing toxins.
  • Reactive Arthritis: Triggered by infection elsewhere in the body. Ayurveda uses cleansing and herbal remedies to balance doshas.
  • Gout: Sudden, painful swelling (often in the big toe) due to uric acid buildup. Ayurveda aims to reduce uric acid and manage pain.

    • Ayurvedic Treatment Approach

  • Ayurvedic Perspective: Arthritis is seen as a result of dosha imbalance (mainly Vata) and toxin (ama) accumulation. Treatment restores balance and eliminates toxins.
  • Dietary Recommendations: Eat anti-inflammatory foods like turmeric, ginger, and garlic. Avoid processed, sugary, and fatty foods.
  • Herbal Remedies: Herbs such as Ashwagandha, Shallaki, and Nirgundi help reduce joint pain and swelling.
  • Therapeutic Treatments: Panchakarma therapies (Abhyanga, Swedana, Basti) detoxify and relieve symptoms.
  • Lifestyle Modifications: Regular exercise, yoga, meditation, rest, and healthy weight maintenance improve mobility and reduce stress.
