What Is Cerebral Palsy?

Cerebral palsy is a group of disorders that affect movement, balance, and posture. It is caused by problems in the brain that control muscles and coordination. The severity of cerebral palsy can vary—some people may need full-time support for daily tasks, while others may manage with little or no help.
Without proper Ayurvedic treatment for cerebral palsy, the condition may worsen over time, leading to more complications. Early care can help manage symptoms and improve the quality of life.

What Are the Causes of Cerebral Palsy?

Cerebral palsy in Ayurveda is treated based on identifying its root causes. The main causes include:

  • Genetic disorders or brain development issues during pregnancy.
  • Infections in the brain that affect its normal functions.
  • Lack of oxygen to the brain during birth or later stages.
  • Severe jaundice in newborns.
  • Head injuries due to accidents or falls.
  • Brain damage affecting areas that control movement and posture.

Most cases develop before or shortly after birth. Understanding the cause helps in offering the right herbal medicine for cerebral palsy.

Types of Cerebral Palsy

The type of cerebral palsy determines the best Ayurvedic treatment plan. Common types include:

  • Spastic Cerebral Palsy: This is the most common form, affecting about 80% of people. It causes stiff muscles and movement issues.
    • Spastic Diplegia: Affects mainly the legs.
    • Spastic Hemiplegia: Affects one side of the body, mostly the arm.
    • Spastic Quadriplegia: The most severe form, affecting the face, arms, legs, and trunk.
  • Dyskinetic Cerebral Palsy: Causes difficulty in sitting and walking. Movements may be slow or jerky, and muscle tone can vary throughout the day.
  • Ataxic Cerebral Palsy: Affects balance and coordination. Children may walk unsteadily and struggle with fast movements.
  • Mixed Cerebral Palsy: Some individuals may show symptoms of more than one type, making the condition more complex.

    • Signs and Symptoms of Cerebral Palsy

  • Floppy or stiff muscle tone
  • Delayed rolling or crawling
  • Favoring one hand while the other remains clenched
  • Poor coordination and imbalance
  • Delayed motor skills like sitting or walking

    • With early Ayurvedic treatment for cerebral palsy in children, these symptoms can be managed, and coordination can improve.

    Complications of Cerebral Palsy

    Delaying treatment for cerebral palsy can lead to complications such as:

  • Seizures (epilepsy)
  • Muscle and bone problems
  • Learning disabilities
  • Difficulty eating and swallowing
  • Vision or hearing problems
  • Speech and communication issues

    • Using a combined approach, including Ayurveda and physiotherapy for cerebral palsy, can help reduce complications and improve outcomes in severe cases.
