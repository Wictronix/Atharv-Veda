What Is Fatty Liver Disease?

Fatty liver disease, also known as hepatic steatosis, occurs when excess fat accumulates in the liver cells. While a small amount of fat in the liver is normal, excessive fat can lead to inflammation and liver damage. There are two primary types: alcoholic fatty liver disease (AFLD) and non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD). NAFLD is more common and often linked to metabolic conditions such as obesity, type 2 diabetes, and high cholesterol.
In the early stages, fatty liver disease may not present noticeable symptoms. However, if left untreated, it can progress to more severe liver conditions, including cirrhosis and liver failure.

Types of Fatty Liver Disease

  • Non-Alcoholic Fatty Liver Disease (NAFLD): Most common type, often linked to obesity, diabetes, and high cholesterol.
  • Alcoholic Fatty Liver Disease (AFLD): Caused by excessive alcohol consumption, leading to fat buildup in the liver.
