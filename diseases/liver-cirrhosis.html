What Is Liver Cirrhosis?

Liver cirrhosis is a serious condition where healthy liver tissue is replaced by scar tissue, reducing the liver’s ability to function properly. This affects essential liver functions like metabolism, filtering toxins, and making proteins needed for blood clotting.
Ayurvedic treatment for liver cirrhosis uses natural herbs, detox therapies, and lifestyle changes to help heal and restore liver health. Ayurveda focuses on balancing the body, mind, and spirit for overall well-being. Liver cirrhosis treatment in Ayurveda includes herbal medicines, stress relief, a proper diet, and cleansing methods to support the liver.

What Causes Liver Cirrhosis?

Several factors can cause liver cirrhosis, and these are carefully evaluated during Atharveda’s liver cirrhosis treatment.

  • Long-term alcohol abuse and Hepatitis C infection are the most common causes.
  • Fatty liver disease can also lead to cirrhosis in some cases.
  • Infections like Hepatitis B are a major cause of liver cirrhosis globally.
  • Some inherited diseases can damage the liver and cause scarring.
  • Constant exposure to toxins or pollutants, such as arsenic, may also damage the liver over time.

    • Ayurveda for liver cirrhosis aims to restore liver function naturally and improve the body’s ability to heal from within.

    Signs and Symptoms of Liver Cirrhosis

    Liver cirrhosis may not show symptoms early on, but as it progresses, the following signs may appear:

  • Spider-like blood vessels (spider angiomas)
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Nausea and digestive discomfort
  • Dark-colored urine
  • Mental confusion or memory problems
  • Swelling in the legs due to fluid retention

    • Ayurvedic medicine for liver cirrhosis helps manage these symptoms and improve liver performance over time. As the condition worsens, more scar tissue builds up, making early and effective treatment essential.

    Complications of Liver Cirrhosis

    • ● Weak immune system
    • ● Hormone imbalance
    • ● Internal bleeding in the digestive system
    • ● Swelling and inflammation from fluid buildup
    • ● End-stage or chronic liver failure
