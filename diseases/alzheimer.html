What Is Alzheimer’s Disease?

Alzheimer’s disease is the most common type of dementia, a condition that slowly affects the brain’s ability to think, remember, and communicate. It begins with mild memory loss and gradually worsens, making it hard for a person to carry out daily tasks. As the disease progresses, the person may lose the ability to respond to their environment or hold a conversation.
Ayurvedic treatment for Alzheimer’s disease focuses on addressing the root causes and managing the symptoms naturally to improve quality of life.

Causes of Alzheimer’s Disease

To choose the most effective Ayurvedic medicine for Alzheimer’s, understanding the underlying causes is important.
One of the major causes is the abnormal build-up of proteins in and around brain cells. These proteins include:

  • Amyloid: Forms plaques around the brain cells
  • Tau: Causes tangles inside brain tissue

These abnormal deposits affect how brain cells communicate and function. Over time, there’s a reduction in important brain chemicals called neurotransmitters, especially acetylcholine, which plays a vital role in memory and learning.
As the disease advances, brain shrinkage occurs, starting with areas that control memory. In some less common forms of Alzheimer’s, different parts of the brain may be affected.
Alzheimer’s can also run in families. If several family members developed dementia at an early age, genetic counselling may be helpful. In some people, such as those with Down syndrome, the risk of developing Alzheimer’s is higher due to genetic factors that cause protein build-up.

Signs and Symptoms of Alzheimer’s Disease

When considering Alzheimer’s Ayurvedic treatment, sharing the symptoms with your practitioner helps in preparing a personalized care plan. Common symptoms include:

  • Memory loss and poor judgment
  • Repeating the same conversations or questions
  • Aphasia (difficulty speaking or understanding speech)
  • Confusion and mood swings
  • Disturbed sleep cycle
  • Hallucinations or speech loss in advanced stages
  • Difficulty swallowing (dysphagia)
  • Urinary incontinence

    • Ayurvedic medicine for Alzheimer’s disease is designed to target these symptoms, improve brain function, and support mental well-being.

    Complications of Alzheimer’s Disease

  • Inability to manage everyday tasks like cooking or handling finances
  • Wandering or getting lost from home
  • Personality changes that strain personal relationships
  • Hallucinations and delusions in the advanced stage
  • Sundowning — a condition where symptoms worsen in the late afternoon or evening, causing agitation, confusion, and restlessness

    • As the condition worsens, individuals with Alzheimer’s eventually need full-time care and supervision to stay safe and comfortable.
