What Is Parkinson's Disease?

Parkinson’s disease is a progressive neurological disorder that affects movement control. It occurs when nerve cells in the brain that produce dopamine, a chemical responsible for smooth and coordinated muscle movements, become damaged or die. This leads to symptoms such as tremors, stiffness, slowness of movement, and balance problems.
While the exact cause is unknown, several factors may contribute to the development of Parkinson’s disease. These include genetic mutations, environmental toxins, and certain medications. The disease typically progresses slowly over time, and its symptoms can vary from person to person.
